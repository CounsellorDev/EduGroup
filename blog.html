How to Study For Exams

by MeetSchool Educational Team• September, 2019 • News

Taking exams is stressful, but you can make it easier by improving the way you study. Studying for your exams effectively and efficiently will keep you from feeling unprepared, and it will set you up for success!

Best Way of Review your syllabus.

by MeetSchool Educational Team• Jan 18, 2019 • News

Figure out when all of your exams will be and how much of your grade they are worth. Put these dates on your calendar or planner so they don't sneak up on you! Plan review sessions beginning at least a week in advance of each exam.

Best way to Pay attention in class.

by MeetSchools Educational Teams• Jan 18, 2019 • News

This seems like a no-brainer, but actually paying attention while you're in class will help you immensely once exam time comes. Don't fall into the trap of thinking you'll just "absorb" knowledge; be an active learner. Try sitting at the front of the class, this will make it easier to focus.

How to Take good notes in classes.

by MeetSchool Educational Team• September, 2019 • News

This is easier said than done, but learning how to take good notes will help you immensely once it comes time to study. Write down everything your teacher writes on the board or puts up in slides. Try to record as much of what the teacher says as possible, but don't allow taking notes to distract you so much that you forget to listen..

Making Study; part of your habits

by MeetSchool Educational Team• Jan 18, 2019 • News

Too often, it's easy to view studying as something that only gets done at the last minute in a huge overnight cram session. Instead, try setting aside some time every day to study. Scheduling it just like another appointment or class may help you stay motivated to continue the habit.[1]

Creating an Optimal Environment for Learning

by MeetSchool Educational Team• Jan 18, 2019 • News

Study in a clean , quiet and orderly room. Keep anything and everything away from where you are that may cause you to get distracted. Jumping up to read a text message on your phone or periodically checking social media is ill-advised whilst studying.

Warning:


  • Avoid stress from other people, if at all possible. Creating an atmosphere of negativity and stress around studying will make you want to avoid it.
  • Don't study only on the night before the exam. Study everything bit by bit when you come home from school every day.
  • It's no use studying everything in one shot.
  • Cheating won't solve your exam problems, it will just end up with getting you busted. There are often severe penalties for cheating, including notes on your official record and even expulsion.
  • Don't stay up too late. Studies show that you are more likely to forget what you learned if you don't get enough sleep, so get enough sleep! If you really need to study more, do some in the morning.
